# Category Decision Guide

## Summary

- Defines canonical guidance for this reference area.
- Use this file to keep story-architect outputs consistent and canon-safe.


Use this section to both identify a category and understand what it represents.

## world

Defines the entire universe and establishes global context.

Classify as `world` when the concept is the top-level lore frame, setting-wide identity, or overarching canon scope.

## region

Defines a place at any scale.

Classify as `region` for realms, regions, states, cities, districts, buildings, shops, landmarks, or any location concept.

## rule

Defines a law of reality or persistent world behavior.

Classify as `rule` when the concept explains how the world consistently works.

## inhabitant

Defines a being, species, creature, or entity.

Classify as `inhabitant` when the concept centers on who exists in the world.

## artifact

Defines an object used in the world.

Classify as `artifact` for crafted objects, tools, relics, currency, or documents.

## phenomenon

Defines a recurring natural or supernatural occurrence.

Classify as `phenomenon` when the concept is an event pattern rather than an object, place, or person.

## culture

Defines a society or belief system.

Classify as `culture` for traditions, norms, worldviews, and collective identity systems.

## symbol

Defines a cultural or visual emblem.

Classify as `symbol` when meaning is carried by a sign, icon, motif, or emblem.

## myth

Defines a legendary narrative.

Classify as `myth` when the entry is a culturally believed story that may not be confirmed as factual.

## story

Defines a narrative fragment or event.

Classify as `story` for scene-like narratives, historical episodes, or storytelling entries.

## artwork

Defines a real-world artwork connected to the world.

Classify as `artwork` when the concept is an external creative work (for example image, print, piece, edition) tied to lore.
